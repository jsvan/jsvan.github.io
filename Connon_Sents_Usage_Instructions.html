Welcome to Julian's Common Sents, the media sentiment collector! I appreciate all your help and I hope you have fun too. Now you can read the news for science.

The main goal is to seamlessly allow you to collect positively or negatively charged sentences from news articles while you read.
We believe language used in the media implicitly gives you queues to differentiate the good guys from the bad guys.
We ask you, to when you see such a sentence used that conveys


Then please mark that sentence as the CONTEXT, and the associated entities as Positive or Negative (or Neutral if relevant) examples.
In short, if the sentence makes you think Less of the place, it's negative, if it makes you respect it more, it's positive.

Try to send an email of the list (and remember to clear it out!) every so often. If you read lots of news, try to send an email every day. The list will continue to exist if you close your browser, or switch webpages. This is not thoroughly tested so we can't guarantee this.

EXAMPLE

Months before the elections, a coalition of three media outlets—Kloop.kg, Radio Azattyk, and the Organized Crime and Corruption Reporting Project—produced a series of investigative reports revealing Matraimov’s funneling abroad of $700 million in cash as part of an elaborate money laundering effort.

The three organizations would be "POSITIVE", and Matraimov would be "NEGATIVE".

General rules

  • Please ignore WHO the phrase about, only take in the immediate context of the sentence please!
  • Please ignore sentences without clearly labeled people.

EXAMPLE 2

"Satan cared for cute sick puppies in his spare time. He otherwise was a bad dude."

In the context of "Satan cared for cute sick puppies in his spare time.", please mark "Satan" as "POSITIVE". The following, "He otherwise was a bad dude." should be ignored, because it is in of itself unclear who "He" refers to.

  • Set the [CONTEXT] to:
    • "Satan cared for cute sick puppies in his spare time."
  • Mark "Satan" as :
    • [POSITIVE]
    Because the sentence makes Satan look good. Pretend you have no idea who Satan otherwise is.
  • Ignore the sentence "He otherwise was a bad dude."

Guide to Navigating the Menus

First, highlight a block of text to be context and right-click it. Then, navigate to the Common Sents menu item, and click on the item that says "Set new context". Alternatively, just highlight the text and hit the keyboard shortcut alt-C



Once a context is set, you can view it in the same right-click menu



Label a text selection with a sentiment by highlighting it, then right clicking on it, and selecting the appropriate sentiment. Alternatively, just highlight it and hit the keyboard shortcut appropriate to that sentiment. Notice that the keyboard shortcuts are listed in the right click menu next to their corresponding actions



Extensions with pin clicked show up on the dashboard



Extensions without pin clicked are accessible in the extensions menu (puzzle piece icon)
